Featured Content (<section>)

Use <section> for grouping related content with a heading. If it could stand alone (e.g., a blog post), consider <article> instead.

Why Semantics Matter

This <article> has its own <header>—totally valid. Articles are portable units.

Semantic HTML clarifies intent for machines and humans. Search engines, screen readers, and your future self will thank you. It doesn’t change visuals by itself; your CSS does that. But semantics shape structure and meaning.

Section vs. Article—Quick Rule of Thumb

Article can be lifted and reused elsewhere without losing meaning (like a standalone blog post). Section is a thematic chunk inside a larger context and usually needs that context to make sense.

If you wouldn’t share it as a separate unit, it’s probably a section, not an article.

Sidebar (<aside>)

Use <aside> for content adjacent to the main flow—related posts, newsletter signup, small tools.
